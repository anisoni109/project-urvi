Who we are

Sustainability is not just what we do. It is who we are.

Project Urvi was founded on the belief that a safer and more sustainable world is not a distant ideal - it is something we build through concrete, research-led action today. Every initiative we take, every service we offer, and every partnership we form is rooted in this commitment.

From climate research focused on the Global South to helping businesses rethink their materials and market strategies, we work across disciplines to drive real, lasting change.

Climate Action

Research and solutions grounded in the realities of climate change across the Global South.

Circular Economy

Turning waste into resource - creating economic value while reducing environmental burden.

Sustainable Business

Helping companies transform their operations, sourcing, and strategy for a sustainable future.

Inclusive Growth

Ensuring sustainability benefits reach people equitably, especially in underserved communities.

Why back Project Urvi

A commercial opportunity with real-world impact.

Project Urvi operates at the intersection of sustainability and business viability - building ventures that generate returns alongside measurable environmental and social outcomes.

Growing Market

Demand for sustainability advisory, circular economy implementation, and environmental compliance support is accelerating across India and South Asia, driven by regulation, ESG mandates, and shifting business expectations. We are positioned early in a large and underserved market.

Dual Impact

Support for Project Urvi creates measurable change across three dimensions - people (better sustainability planning, livelihoods, and local implementation capacity), animals (less unmanaged waste and pollution entering ecosystems), and nature (healthier soils, waterways, and material systems through more responsible environmental practice).

Small Recycling Unit Partnership

We are actively looking for partners, supporters, and early backers to help set up a small plastic recycling unit. Support would go toward equipment, collection logistics, local operating capacity, and the partnerships needed to build a credible pilot that can later scale responsibly.
