Why back Project Urvi

A commercial opportunity with real-world impact.

Project Urvi operates at the intersection of sustainability and business viability - building ventures that generate returns alongside measurable environmental and social outcomes.

Growing Market

Demand for recycled materials and sustainable business services is accelerating across India and South Asia, driven by regulation, ESG mandates, and shifting consumer expectations. We are positioned early in a large and underserved market.

Dual Impact

Capital deployed through Project Urvi creates measurable change across three dimensions - people (livelihoods and health in communities throughout the recycling value chain), animals (less plastic entering rivers, wetlands, and ecosystems that wildlife depend on), and nature (healthier soils, waterways, and forests through reduced plastic pollution and more responsible industry practice).

Use of Investment

Funds are deployed directly toward expanding plastic recovery operations, building consultancy and research capacity, and developing the infrastructure to serve a growing base of industrial buyers and sustainability clients across industry and government.

Research & publications

Knowledge that drives sustainable action.

Our team conducts and publishes research on climate change, circular economy, sustainability policy, and the Global South. Below are selected works from our founders and researchers.

Energy Policy 2024

National Green Hydrogen Mission

Mohit Kumar  ·  Ed. Riya Pawar

A policy analysis of India's National Green Hydrogen Mission - examining its goals to produce 5 MMT of green hydrogen annually by 2030, decarbonise hard-to-abate sectors, and position India as a global clean energy hub, alongside key implementation challenges and prospects.

Read publication ↗
Climate Policy 2024

Green India Mission

Soham Chakraborty  ·  Ed. Riya Pawar

An evaluation of India's National Mission for a Green India (GIM) under the NAPCC - assessing its achievements in forest cover expansion, community-led forest management, and biodiversity conservation, alongside persistent challenges in implementation and ecological quality.

Read publication ↗
Climate Change 2024

Climate Refugees: The World's Forgotten Victims

Tanavi Gumma  ·  Ed. Riya Pawar

An examination of climate-induced displacement - who climate refugees are, why they lack legal protection under international law, and how rising sea levels, drought, and extreme weather are driving an accelerating migration crisis that could displace 150-200 million people by 2050.
